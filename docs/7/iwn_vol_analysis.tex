\section{Executive Summary}
% ============================================================

IWN (iShares Russell 2000 Value ETF) trades at \textbf{17.84\% realized vol}
versus BTC's \textbf{67.76\%}---a 3.8$\times$ vol discount. Its 3-month
rolling correlation to BTC is \textbf{--0.028}, essentially zero, making it
an efficient diversifier for a BTC-heavy portfolio. ATM 3-month puts cost
only \textbf{3.998\% of spot} (\$7.95 on \$198.95), roughly one-quarter the
cost of equivalent CRWD protection (15.68\%).

This note evaluates whether IWN vol is cheap relative to its own history,
sizes a protective put overlay for our current portfolio, and outlines the
audit pipeline that will read live pricing from Redis once the feed service
deploys.

IWN's 17.84\% vol is \textbf{7.4\% below IWM} (the blend-weighted Russell 2000),
reflecting the value-factor tilt's dampening effect. Historically, Russell 2000
Value has traded in the 15--25\% vol range, with spikes to 35--40\% in stress
regimes (Q1 2020, Q4 2018). The current reading sits in the \textbf{lower
tercile}---suggesting either a genuinely low-vol environment or a compression
that may revert.

\subsection{Implied vs.\ Realized Spread}

Using our Black--Scholes pricing with 17.84\% realized vol and $r = 4.5\%$,
the 3-month ATM put prices at \textbf{3.998\% of spot}. If exchange-listed
implied vol is materially above realized (as is typical for small-cap indices
due to skew), the market is pricing in a vol premium. If implied $\approx$
realized, \textbf{puts are cheap relative to the risk they hedge}.

\section{Audit Pipeline (Pending Deployment)}

The \texttt{audit/} module is ready to consume live data once the Redis feed
service deploys:

\begin{enumerate}[nosep]
  \item \texttt{reader.py} reads \texttt{options:\{OCC\}} hashes and hydrates
        the \texttt{OptionsRecord} protobuf (identity, pricing, greeks, sizing,
        orders, bars).
  \item \texttt{checks.py} runs six audit checks per record:
        \begin{itemize}[nosep]
          \item \textbf{Identity} --- required fields populated
          \item \textbf{Staleness} --- data age $<$ 5 minutes
          \item \textbf{Pricing sanity} --- bid $\leq$ mid $\leq$ ask
          \item \textbf{Spread} --- bid-ask $<$ 10\% (liquidity)
          \item \textbf{Greeks} --- IV and delta present
          \item \textbf{Order limits} --- open order drift $<$ 20\%
        \end{itemize}
  \item CLI: \texttt{python -m audit IWN... CRWD... --json}
\end{enumerate}

Once live, we will:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item Compare \textbf{implied vol} (from Redis greeks) vs.\ \textbf{realized vol}
        to confirm whether IWN puts are truly cheap.
  \item Track the \textbf{IV term structure} across 1M/3M/6M tenors.
  \item Monitor \textbf{rolling correlation stability} to detect regime shifts.
\end{itemize}
