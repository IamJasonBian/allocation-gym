\section{Executive Summary}
% ============================================================

This report summarizes using \textbf{Grayscale BTC Mini Trust} (ticker BTC,
\$32.35/share) and \textbf{IWM put options} to maintain a position for
capturing long-term macro momentum while trading intra-week volatility.

The portfolio maintains a 46.1\% BTC Mini Trust position with
\textcolor{gain}{+\$15,770} in BTC trading P\&L and
\textcolor{gain}{+\$4,195} in options P\&L over the Feb~21--Mar~4 window.
A full-history backtest (398 trading days) and thesis decay analysis reveal
correlations between BTC and major indices averaging $+0.45$ to $+0.53$ (63d).

This strategy enables long-term holds for volatile assets as a risk management
framework with trending momentum, capturing gains via momentum-based DCA
during intra-week drawdowns. The approach could serve as a systematic arbitrage strategy by
exploiting correlated drawdowns across BTC and small-cap equities.
Additionally, the strategy seeks to identify where \textbf{volatility is
underpriced relative to institutional demand}---IWM puts at 4.4\% of spot
(3M ATM) and SPY puts at 2.6\% provide significantly cheaper protection
than direct BTC puts at 14.0\%.

\subsection{Grayscale BTC Mini Trust}

Grayscale BTC Mini Trust is a low-cost (\$0.15 expense ratio) alternative to
owning BTC directly. It trades on public markets during regular hours
(9:30am--4:00pm ET) and on some after-hours exchanges. Key characteristics:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Current position}: 2,613 shares at \$32.23 = \$84,228
        (43.2\% of portfolio)
  \item \textbf{Tracks spot BTC}: $\rho = +0.97$ (21d) with BTC/USD,
        $+1.00$ with IBIT (spot BTC ETF)
  \item \textbf{Realized vol}: 81.5\% annualized (21d) vs.\ 83.0\% for
        spot BTC/USD
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Disadvantages}: slippage during after-hours and pre-market trading
(the trust must track to BTC/USD with limited liquidity). It is also
difficult to rebalance and de-risk BTC/USD exposure over the weekend when
crypto markets trade but equity markets are closed.

\subsection{Rolling Return Correlations}

In the midst of recent market volatility, BTC Mini Trust has exhibited strong
correlation at a macro level with popular indices and individual stocks. We
compute the Pearson correlation on log returns:
\[
  \rho_{N}(t) = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{N}(r^{A}_i - \bar{r}^{A})(r^{B}_i - \bar{r}^{B})}
  {\sqrt{\sum(r^{A}_i - \bar{r}^{A})^2}\;\sqrt{\sum(r^{B}_i - \bar{r}^{B})^2}}
  \quad\text{where } r_i = \ln\!\left(\frac{P_i}{P_{i-1}}\right)
\]

\subsection{Call Trading Variant}

The same correlation structure supports \textbf{covered call strategies}:
\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{IWM covered calls}: sell OTM calls against IWM shares
        to generate income during low-vol periods. The $+0.77$ BTC
        correlation means IWM rallies when BTC rallies, providing a
        natural offset.
  \item \textbf{BTC bracket + IWM call spread}: pair the BTC bracket
        strategy with IWM bull call spreads to capture correlated upside
        at lower premium than direct BTC calls (vol discount).
  \item \textbf{Single-stock call overlays}: CRWD, NVDA, or other
        high-beta names where implied vol exceeds realized---sell
        premium on the call side during mean-reversion regimes.
\end{itemize}

\section{Monitoring}
% ============================================================

Ongoing signals to track for strategy calibration:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Implied vs.\ realized vol}: compare IWM/IWN implied vol
        (from options chain) against 21d realized vol. When IV $>$ RV by
        $>$20\%, puts are expensive---favor selling. When IV $<$ RV,
        puts are cheap---favor buying.
  \item \textbf{BTC implied vol}: track Deribit DVOL or BTC options
        skew. When BTC IV is elevated relative to IWM IV, the
        cost advantage of IWM puts increases.
  \item \textbf{Rolling correlation stability}: monitor 21d BTC$\leftrightarrow$IWM
        correlation. If $\rho$ drops below $+0.50$, the hedge
        effectiveness degrades and position sizing should be reduced.
  \item \textbf{Candidate screening}: scan for additional hedge
        candidates where $\rho > +0.60$ with BTC and 3M ATM put cost
        $< 6\%$ of spot.
\end{itemize}
