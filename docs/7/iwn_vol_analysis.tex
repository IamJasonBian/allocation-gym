\section{Implied Volatility Smile}
% ============================================================

The \textbf{implied volatility smile} (or ``smirk'') is one of the most
important features of equity options markets. It describes how implied
volatility varies across strikes for a given expiration, deviating from the
constant-vol assumption of the Black-Scholes model.

\subsection{Theory}

In the Black-Scholes framework, all options on the same underlying with the
same expiration should have identical implied volatility regardless of strike.
In practice, this is never observed. Instead:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Equity index options} exhibit a \textit{volatility skew}
        (also called a ``smirk''): OTM puts have higher IV than ATM options,
        which in turn have higher IV than OTM calls. This reflects demand for
        downside protection and the empirical fat-tailed, negatively-skewed
        distribution of equity returns.
  \item \textbf{Single-stock options} may show a more symmetric smile,
        especially around earnings events where large moves in either
        direction are expected.
  \item \textbf{Small-cap ETFs like IWN} tend to exhibit a pronounced skew
        similar to broad indices, as institutional hedging demand concentrates
        in OTM puts.
\end{itemize}

The smile can be characterised by its level (ATM IV), slope (the put-call
skew), and curvature (how quickly IV increases away from ATM in both
directions). Formally, for a fixed expiry $T$:
\[
  \sigma_{\mathrm{imp}}(K) = \sigma_{\mathrm{ATM}} + b_1\left(\frac{K - S}{S}\right)
  + b_2\left(\frac{K - S}{S}\right)^2 + \cdots
\]
where $b_1 < 0$ captures the skew (puts more expensive) and $b_2 > 0$
captures the curvature (both wings elevated).

\subsection{IV Term Structure}

The \textbf{term structure of implied volatility} describes how IV varies
across expiration dates at a fixed moneyness level. A ``normal'' term
structure is upward-sloping (longer-dated options have higher IV), while
an inverted term structure can signal near-term event risk or elevated
uncertainty.

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.85\textwidth]{iwn_iv_term_structure.png}
  \caption{IWN average IV by expiration date, comparing early March and late

\end{figure}

\subsection{Interpreting the Smile for Hedging}

The IV smile has direct implications for the put hedging strategy:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Skew cost}: OTM put protection costs more (in IV terms) than
        ATM. For IWN, the put-call IV skew adds
        ${\raise.17ex\hbox{$\scriptstyle\sim$}}$2--5pp to OTM put IV relative
        to OTM calls, increasing hedge cost.
  \item \textbf{ATM vs OTM trade-off}: ATM puts provide better vol-adjusted
        value (lower IV) but cost more in dollar terms. OTM puts are cheaper
        in dollars but embed a skew premium.
  \item \textbf{Vega risk}: When the smile steepens (skew increases), existing
        put positions gain value even without a move in the underlying. This
        creates a secondary source of P\&L beyond pure delta hedging.
  \item \textbf{Calendar spread opportunity}: if near-term IV is elevated
        relative to longer-dated IV (inverted term structure), selling
        short-dated puts and buying longer-dated puts captures the term
        structure normalisation.
\end{itemize}

Ongoing signals to track for strategy calibration:

\begin{itemize}[nosep]
  \item \textbf{Implied vs.\ realized vol}: compare IWM/IWN implied vol
        (from options chain) against 21d realized vol. When IV $>$ RV by
        $>$20\%, puts are expensive---favor selling. When IV $<$ RV,
        puts are cheap---favor buying.
  \item \textbf{IV smile shape}: monitor the put-call skew and smile
        curvature over time. A steepening skew signals increasing demand
        for downside protection and may precede market drawdowns.
  \item \textbf{BTC implied vol}: track Deribit DVOL or BTC options
        skew. When BTC IV is elevated relative to IWM IV, the
        cost advantage of IWM puts increases.
  \item \textbf{Rolling correlation stability}: monitor 21d BTC$\leftrightarrow$IWM
        correlation. If $\rho$ drops below $+0.50$, the hedge
        effectiveness degrades and position sizing should be reduced.
  \item \textbf{Term structure slope}: an inverted IV term structure
        (near-term IV $>$ long-term IV) signals heightened near-term risk
        and opportunities for calendar spread hedges.
\end{itemize}
